1) Haritha Kanda,Bogawanthalawa,Sri Lanka

Location
Click here to view on Map
Introduction:
Haritha Kanda is an excellent destination for beginners looking to experience hiking and camping. This guide provides essential information to help you plan your adventure.
Route:
Start from Colombo and proceed to Avissawella > Kithulgala > Ginigathhena > Bogawanthalawa > Bogawana Estate. Upon arrival at Bagowanthalawa, local three-wheel drivers can guide you to the starting point of the hike.
Transportation:
Options include bikes, three-wheelers, and public transport.
Weather:
The weather can be windy and misty. Mornings are particularly cold, so ensure you have warm clothing.
Water:
While water sources are available, it’s recommended to bring your own drinking water or a water filter.
Hiking Guide:
The hike is approximately 45 minutes long along a clear path. Local residents can provide directions if needed.

2) Chariot Path of King Rawana,Pussallawa Udapalatha,Kandy,Sri Lanka

Location
Click here to view on Map
Introduction:
The chariot path is the route which Ravana transported Sita Devi on his chariot from Sita Kotuwa to Ashoka Vatika also known as Ravana grass pathway which is located jungle on the top of Ramboda Hills on the Kandy – Nuwara Eliya main road. This is one of the iconic Ramayana sites in Sri Lanka. The Sita tear pond is a small pond that can be found en-route by the chariot path. A pond located on this Chariot Path is believed to have been formed here by the tears of Sita Devi and has not dried up so far even during severe droughts when the adjoining rivers do. It is located on top of a mountain range in the central highlands of Sri Lanka which has spectacular views from an angle of 360 degrees around.
Route:
Starting your journey from Colombo, you will first need to reach Gampola. From Gampola, take a bus to Pussallawa village, where you should hire a car capable of handling rough roads to get to Frotoft estate/Perettasi village, a trip that takes about 1.5 to 2 hours. Buses are an alternative, but they are infrequent. Upon arrival, locate the old hospital facility where the trailhead begins. The hike through the tea estate is relatively easy, but the climb along the footpath is more challenging and takes 2 to 3 hours. Along the way, you'll enjoy scenic views of Kotmale Reservoir, Pidurangala, Hanguranketha, Sri Padaya, and Hewaheta, making it worthwhile to spend at least half a day exploring this Ramayana site. Be prepared for chilly weather and wind, especially at night, and wear long-sleeved clothing to protect yourself from the spiky grass. Note that this journey is not suitable for senior citizens or those with heart or respiratory issues.
Weather:
Most of the year, the Chariot Path receives rainfall. The temperature ranges from 10°C to 24°C. Between October and January, heavy monsoon rains fall. The months of February through April are ideal for visiting the Chariot Path. Between these months, the majority of the days are bright, with the least amount of rain.

While water sources are available.

3) Kabaragala,Nawalapitiya, Sri Lanka

Location
Click here to view on Map
Introduction:
Kabaragala is the highest mountain peak within the Dolosbage range, soaring approximately 1506 meters above sea level
Route:
Your exploration of Kabaragala kicks off at the Maryville Junction. As you embark on this journey, you traverse through a variety of terrains, each contributing to the charm of the adventure. The expedition starts with a stop at a Kovil, then proceeds along a concrete road leading to the mountain. As you step into a tea estate, the path becomes narrower, funneling you onto a slim but clearly marked trail. As you advance, a quaint forest filled with pine trees welcomes you. After about an hour of hiking, you arrive at the first viewpoint – a spot that offers breathtaking views of the landscape around you. This is a moment that truly rewards the effort of the trek. While the first viewpoint marks a common stopping point for many hikers, the true beauty of Kabaragala awaits those who venture further. The terrain closely mirrors Horton Plains, showcasing mana trees and savanna-type plants. The remarkable drop towards the bottom, reminiscent of the world's end, adds an element of thrill and wonder. After an invigorating two-hour hike, you'll stand triumphant at the summit of Kabaragala Mountain. The view from this vantage point is nothing short of breathtaking. The panorama encompasses the Kothmale Reservoir, Peacock Hills, Kothmale Area, Dolosbage Area, Pusellawa, Ambuluwawa, and the iconic Sri Pada Mountain. Additionally, you'll be able to gaze upon lesser-known mountains and quaint villages. Photographers will find themselves in paradise, especially during the early morning when mist blankets the landscape. Keep an eye out for the captivating sight of clouds nestled beneath the mountains, a signature feature of Kabaragala.
Transportation:
Reaching Kabaragala is an adventure with two primary routes for exploration. One starts from Nawalapitiya, while the other originates from the Gampola side. The distances to Kabaragala vary: 11 km from Colombo, 40 km from Kandy, 24 km from Gampola, and 20 km from Nawalapitiya. If you're coming from Colombo, the route passes through Avissawella and Ginigathhena to Nawalapitiya. For those starting from Kandy, Gampola is your gateway. Public transport is readily available at the hike's starting point. By taking the Dolosbage bus from either bus stop, you can expect an hour's journey to reach the hiking trail's entrance.
Water:
water sources are not available in campsite, it’s recommended to bring your own drinking water or a water filter.
Hiking Guide:
The hike is approximately 1 hour long along a clear path. Local residents can provide directions if needed.

4) Jungle Beach, Unawatuna, Sri Lanka

Location
Click here to view on Map
Introduction:
Jungle Beach is a picturesque bay surrounded by lush jungle, located northwest of Unawatuna in the Southern Province of Sri Lanka. It’s an ideal spot for sunbathing and snorkeling
Route:
Tuk-Tuk Ride: Take a tuk-tuk (a three-wheeler taxi) from Unawatuna to the Jungle Beach path. The tuk-tuk will drop you off at the starting point of the trail.
Jungle Beach Path: From the starting point, follow the Jungle Beach path. It’s a scenic walk through lush greenery and jungle. Be prepared for some uphill sections, as the path can be steep in places.
Enjoy the Beach: Once you reach Jungle Beach, you’ll be rewarded with a beautiful bay surrounded by jungle. Relax on the sandy beach, swim in the clear waters, and explore the reef for snorkeling.
Water:
water sources are not available in campsite, it’s recommended to bring your own drinking water or a water filter.

5) Wangedigala, Kalupahana, Sri Lanka

Location
Click here to view on Map
Introduction:
Wangedigala is a one of famous hikers destination in srilanka which is located between badulla colombo route near kalupahana area The trek to Wangedigala offers adventurers a diverse landscape, leading them through pine forests, verdant grasslands, and cascading waterfalls, culminating in a breathtaking panoramic vista from the summit. Esteemed by locals, this hike demands considerable stamina, typically spanning 3 to 4 hours for the average hiker. Moreover, Wangedigala's cool climate and awe-inspiring views make it a sought-after camping spot, enticing outdoor enthusiasts to bask in its natural splendor.
Route:
Starting the trek from Kalupahana Weli Oya Bridge:
This is the easiest accessible route by public transport since you can start the trek from Weli Oya bridge which is located closer to the Colombo – Badulla Main Route and closer to Kalupahana1. Starting the trek from Bambarakanda falls:
This is the most famous route. The trail head is located approximately 1km towards Kalupahana from Bambarakanda. If you are traveling from Bambaraknda, after 750m towards Kalupahana, you have to take a sharp turn to your right hand side. Then you will see a staircase on your right hand side. That’s where you have to start descending until you find the trail head1. From Belihuloya, you need to travel approximately 14km along the Colombo – Batticaloa Highway & then about 3.2km along the Kalupahana – Ohiya Road to reach Wangedigala

water sources are available in campsite. In the campsite go down near the 57 pile. it’s recommended to bring your own drinking water for the hiking part
